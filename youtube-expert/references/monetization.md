## YouTube Partner Program (YPP) tiers

YouTube Partner Program has two tiers with different thresholds and benefits.

### Tier 1 — Fan Funding (early access)

**Requirements:**
- 500+ subscribers
- 3+ public uploads in last 90 days
- One of:
  - 3,000 watch hours in last 12 months, OR
  - 3 million Shorts views in last 90 days

**Unlocks:**
- Channel memberships
- Super Chat
- Super Stickers
- Super Thanks
- YouTube Shopping (product linking)

### Tier 2 — Full Monetization (ad revenue)

**Requirements:**
- 1,000+ subscribers
- One of:
  - 4,000 watch hours in last 12 months, OR
  - 10 million Shorts views in last 90 days

**Unlocks (in addition to Tier 1 features):**
- AdSense ad revenue
- YouTube Premium revenue share
- All Tier 1 features

**Additional requirements for both tiers:**
- Linked AdSense account
- Two-factor authentication enabled on Google account
- Comply with YouTube monetization policies and Community Guidelines
- Channel must not have active Community Guidelines strikes
- Available in 120+ countries

## YPP application process

1. Go to **studio.youtube.com → Earn** tab
2. Click "Apply now" when threshold is met
3. Review and accept YouTube Partner Program terms
4. Connect (or create) an AdSense account
5. YouTube reviews the channel (typically 1–4 weeks)
6. Receive acceptance or rejection email

**If rejected:** Channels may reapply after 30 days. Common rejection reasons:
- Content violates monetization policies (repetitive content, reused content without transformation, spam)
- Channel doesn't have sufficient original content
- Inactive channel

## Ad revenue details

### Ad types

| Type | Length | Skippable | Notes |
|------|--------|-----------|-------|
| Skippable in-stream | 12 sec–10 min | After 5 sec | Must be watched ≥30 sec (or full if <30 sec) to generate revenue |
| Non-skippable in-stream | 15–20 sec | No | Higher CPM; shown on videos ≥10 minutes |
| Bumper | ≤6 sec | No | Very short; shown before video; usually paired with in-stream |
| Overlay | Text/image banner | N/A | Desktop only; appears over lower portion of video |
| Display | Sidebar image | N/A | Desktop only; beside the video |
| Sponsored cards | Info overlay | N/A | Link to products/playlists within video |

**Mid-roll ads**: Videos at least **8 minutes** long can have mid-roll ads inserted. Mid-rolls generally increase total revenue per video by 50–100%+ on longer content. YouTube can place them automatically or you can set timestamps manually.

### Revenue share
- YouTube takes **45%** of ad revenue
- Creator keeps **55%**
- YouTube Premium: creator's share is proportional to Premium subscribers' watch time on their channel

### CPM factors (what affects your CPM)

| Factor | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| Niche/topic | Finance, legal, B2B: $10–50+ CPM; entertainment: $1–5 CPM |
| Viewer geography | US, UK, AU, CA: highest CPM; developing markets: low CPM |
| Time of year | Q4 (Oct–Dec): highest; Q1 (Jan): lowest |
| Viewer demographics | 25–44 age group earns highest CPM |
| Video length | Longer videos = more ad slots = more revenue |
| Ad type enabled | More ad types = more opportunities |
| Advertiser-friendly content | Controversial topics may have limited ads |

### Self-certification (advertiser-friendliness)
Before publishing, YouTube asks you to self-certify content suitability:
- Not suitable for advertisers: violence, profanity, controversial politics, mature themes
- Self-certifying honestly enables maximum ad revenue; miscertification can lead to account penalties
- "Limited ads" = content is monetized but at reduced CPM due to topic sensitivity

## Fan funding features

### Super Chat
- Available during live streams
- Viewers pay to pin a highlighted message in live chat
- Price tiers from $0.99 to $500
- Duration of pinning scales with payment amount
- Earnings appear in Revenue tab of Analytics

### Super Stickers
- Animated stickers purchased during live streams
- Work alongside Super Chat; no message required
- Multiple sticker packs at different price points

### Super Thanks
- One-time tips on regular (non-live) videos
- Appears as a highlighted comment on the video
- Available in $2, $5, $10, $50 tiers (varies by region)
- Creator must enable per-video or channel-wide in Studio

### Best practices for fan funding
- Acknowledge Super Chat/Super Thanks on stream or in video comments — increases repeat purchases
- Run regular live streams to maximize Super Chat revenue
- Create a community around the channel before expecting fan funding — casual viewers don't tip; loyal fans do
- Promote fan funding during natural pauses, not over your primary content

## Channel memberships

Monthly recurring subscriptions from viewers. Price set by the channel (minimum $0.99/month; common tiers: $1.99, $4.99, $9.99).

**Membership perks you can offer:**
- Custom badges (appear next to name in comments/chat)
- Custom emoji (use in chat and comments)
- Members-only posts (Community tab)
- Members-only videos or early access
- Custom shoutouts

**YouTube's cut:** YouTube takes 30% of membership revenue.

**Best practices:**
- Offer at least 3 tiers with clearly differentiated perks
- Members-only content must feel genuinely exclusive
- Promote membership in live streams (where chat engagement is high)
- Acknowledge members publicly — recognition increases retention
- Regularly update perks to reduce churn

## YouTube Shopping

Link products to your videos and channel. Two modes:

**Your own store:**
- Connect Shopify, WooCommerce, Spring, or other integrated platforms
- Products appear in a shopping shelf below your video
- Also appear in a channel store tab

**Affiliate program:**
- Link products from brands you don't own
- Earn commission when viewers purchase through your links
- Works even if you don't have your own store

**Best practices:**
- Only promote products you'd genuinely recommend — trust is the asset
- Mention products naturally within the video and point to the description/shelf
- Tag products directly in the video at the relevant timestamp

## YPP activity requirements (2025)

YouTube introduced activity-based monetization continuation rules:

| Inactivity period | Consequence |
|------------------|-------------|
| 30 days without uploading | Warning email sent |
| 60 days without uploads | Risk of losing Super Chat and channel memberships |
| 90 days without uploads | Monetization may be suspended entirely |

**Community posts count as activity.** A post every 2–3 weeks during upload hiatuses can prevent monetization loss.

## Revenue optimization tips

1. **Enable all ad types** — every ad type disabled is revenue left on the table; start with all enabled and adjust only if your audience reacts negatively

2. **Keep videos ≥8 minutes** when content justifies it — mid-rolls roughly double revenue per video

3. **Target high-CPM geographies** in your SEO — US/UK/AU/CA audiences are worth 5–10× developing market audiences per view

4. **Publish Q4 content in October** — advertiser demand peaks in November/December; content published in October is indexed and ranked by the time high-CPM spend occurs

5. **Diversify revenue streams** — ad revenue alone is volatile; combine ads + memberships + Super Chat + Shopping to smooth income

6. **Build a loyal community first, then monetize** — forcing fan funding on a disengaged audience fails; authentic community engagement drives voluntary support

7. **Never sacrifice audience trust for revenue** — sponsored content, affiliate links, and product promotions that don't match your audience's interests destroy long-term channel value faster than any short-term revenue gained
