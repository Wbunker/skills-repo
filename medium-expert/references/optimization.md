# Writing Strategy & Growth on Medium

## What Actually Drives Growth (2025–2026)

The algorithm no longer prioritizes follower counts. What matters:
- **Reading completion** — stories people finish rank and earn more
- **Member engagement** — claps, highlights, replies from paying members
- **Boost eligibility** — high-quality stories get amplified regardless of your audience size
- **External traffic** — bringing non-Medium readers in (5% earnings bonus + conversion potential)

---

## Story Length & Format

| Factor | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| Optimal read time | **4–6 minutes** (sweet spot for algorithm and completion rates) |
| Minimum for Boost | ~4+ minutes (thin content rarely gets Boosted) |
| Structure | Headers, short paragraphs, no walls of text |
| Images | Cover image recommended; relevant images improve engagement |
| Subtitle | Use it — appears in previews and search results |

---

## Headlines

Headlines are the single highest-leverage element — they determine click-through rate.

**Good headlines:**
- Specific and honest about what the reader will get
- Create genuine curiosity without deception
- Include the key insight or benefit
- Work as a standalone sentence

**Avoid:**
- Sensationalistic / tabloid tone ("You Won't Believe...")
- Overly generic ("A Guide to...")
- Formulaic listicle titles ("7 Things...")
- Mysterious without payoff ("This Changed Everything")

Medium curators explicitly penalize these patterns when assessing Boost eligibility.

---

## Tags

- Use all **5 available tags** per story
- Tags are the primary discovery mechanism on Medium
- Mix: 1–2 broad tags (large audience) + 3–4 niche tags (targeted readers)
- Check tag follower counts via Unofficial API: `GET /tag/{tag}` returns follower count
- Follow your own topic tags to understand what's trending

---

## Publishing Frequency

- **Minimum for growth**: 2 stories/week
- **Optimal**: 4+ stories/week for faster audience building
- Consistency matters more than occasional viral posts
- Build a backlog so you can publish consistently even during busy periods

---

## Publications Strategy

Publishing in established publications is the fastest way to grow:
1. Start with medium-sized publications (easier to get accepted; still meaningful audience)
2. Build track record → pitch larger publications
3. Target publications in the Boost Nomination Program for distribution upside
4. Don't rely solely on one publication — diversify to build your own follower base too

---

## External Traffic & Distribution

External traffic now earns a **5% bonus** per read from outside Medium (since Oct 2025).

High-value external sources:
- **Email newsletter** — your own list; highest intent readers
- **Twitter/X / LinkedIn** — share headlines + Friend Links for non-members
- **SEO** — Medium pages rank on Google; optimize titles for search terms
- **Pinterest** — surprisingly strong traffic driver for lifestyle/self-improvement content
- **Reddit** — niche subreddits respond well to genuinely helpful content (no spam)

**Friend Links:** Generate a Friend Link (share menu → Get Friend Link) to let non-members read paywalled stories for free. Use strategically to seed shares without permanently making a story free.

---

## Cross-Posting & Canonical URLs

If you publish elsewhere (your own blog, Substack, etc.) and want to also post on Medium:
- Set the **canonical URL** to your original publication when creating the story in Medium
- This tells search engines the original source is elsewhere, preventing SEO duplicate content issues
- Medium accepts cross-posts with canonical URLs — they still earn through the Partner Program

---

## Building an Audience Off-Platform

Medium can restrict your reach if the algorithm changes. Protect yourself:
- Build an **email list** outside Medium (Substack, ConvertKit, etc.)
- Link to your email signup from every story (in bio or within content)
- Medium followers are valuable but don't give you direct contact — email does

---

## Story Checklist Before Publishing

- [ ] Headline is specific, honest, and curiosity-generating (not clickbait)
- [ ] Subtitle adds context and appears in search previews
- [ ] Cover image is relevant and high quality
- [ ] All 5 tags used (mix of broad and niche)
- [ ] Read time is 4–6 minutes
- [ ] Story is enrolled in Partner Program (if monetizing)
- [ ] Submitted to a relevant publication (or published independently with strong tags)
- [ ] Friend Link generated for external sharing (if paywalled)
- [ ] Canonical URL set (if cross-posting from another platform)
