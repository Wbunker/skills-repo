## Content Formats (Ranked by Reach)

| Format | Reach | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Document/PDF Carousel | Highest | Users swipe slides; dwell-time signals amplify. 8–15 slides optimal. |
| Polls | Very high | 200%+ above average. Use genuinely relevant industry questions. |
| Text posts | High | 900–1,500 chars optimal. Story-driven, question-ending. |
| LinkedIn Live | High | Drives notifications to followers. Requires third-party streaming software. |
| Images / Infographics | Moderate | Infographics and quote cards outperform raw photos. |
| Native Video | Low (declining) | Experienced ~200% reach drop in 2025; LinkedIn de-emphasizing in feed. |
| Articles | Low immediate, long shelf life | Indexed by Google. Builds credibility for SSI "Professional Brand" pillar. |
| Newsletters | Compounding | Subscribers auto-notified. Email open-rate analytics added Feb 2025. |
| Audio Events | Niche | Live audio only; no video required. |

## Algorithm Signals

**Amplification signals (ranked by impact):**
1. Comments (~15x weight of likes) — most powerful signal
2. Dwell time — how long someone reads/scrolls your post (carousels win here)
3. Saves — strong value signal, underrated
4. Shares with added commentary — better than bare reshares
5. Reactions — positive but lower weight than comments
6. Profile views generated by the post — secondary amplification signal
7. Early engagement velocity — first 90 minutes determine amplification trajectory
8. Topical relevance to viewer's interest graph — consistent subject matter expands reach beyond network

**Demotion signals (avoid):**
- External links in the post body (move to first comment — ~30% reach reduction)
- Engagement bait phrases ("Comment YES if you agree")
- "Follow me for more X" language
- More than 5 tagged people who aren't likely to engage
- Posts flagged by automated quality filter (generic AI-generated content targeted by March 2026 authenticity update)
- Hashtag stuffing (use 3–5 targeted hashtags max)

**The 90-minute window:** LinkedIn's algorithm decides within the first 90 minutes whether to amplify a post. Seeding early, genuine comments from engaged connections in this window is the highest-leverage tactic.

**Interest-graph distribution (2025–2026 shift):** LinkedIn now scans post text to categorize topics and serves content to users interested in those topics — even outside your network. Clear, consistent writing on a specific subject matter outperforms broad or generic posting.

## Posting Strategy

**Cadence:** 2–5 posts per week with strong engagement outperforms daily posting with mediocre engagement. Algorithm measures engagement rate, not volume.

**Optimal timing:** Tuesday–Thursday, 8–10 AM and 12–2 PM in your audience's time zone. Tuesday shows highest engagement.

**What performs well:**
- Document/PDF carousels with visual design (each slide delivers standalone value)
- Specific, story-driven personal posts — failure stories and lessons outperform success stories
- Data-backed insights or original frameworks
- Question-based posts inviting genuine opinion (not engagement bait)
- Timely commentary on trending industry news

**What gets suppressed:**
- External links in body (use first comment instead)
- Generic AI-sounding content
- Posts with excessive irrelevant tagging
- Engagement bait and "follow me" language

## Post Structure

```
[Hook — one punchy line before "see more" fold]

[Line break]

[Body — short paragraphs, 1–2 sentences each for mobile readability]

[White space between paragraphs — walls of text get scrolled past]

[Specific question to drive comments at the end]

[3–5 targeted hashtags]
```

- First ~200 characters visible before "See more" — make the hook count
- Optimal total length: 900–1,500 characters
- Move external links to the first comment after posting

## Content Mix Formula

| Type | % of Posts | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| Educational | 60% | How-to guides, frameworks, tips, data insights |
| Thought leadership/opinion | 30% | Industry takes, contrarian views, lessons learned |
| Engagement/community | 10% | Polls, questions, event announcements |

**Newsletters (if using):** Post one issue per week or biweekly on a consistent day. Subscribers are auto-notified. Cross-promote newsletter in feed posts to grow subscribers.

**LinkedIn Live (if using):** Announce 48–72 hours in advance. Use pre-event content (polls, teasers) in the event chat to warm the audience. Post recording replay as a post afterward.
