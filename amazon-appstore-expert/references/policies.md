# Amazon Appstore: Policies

## Policy Center

Main reference: https://developer.amazon.com/docs/policy-center/understanding-content-policy.html

Amazon maintains a **family-friendly environment** by default. Policy violations result in app rejection or removal from the Appstore.

**Important**: Metadata (titles, descriptions, images, keywords) must also comply with content policies — non-compliant metadata can cause app suppression even if the app itself is acceptable.

---

## Content Policy Overview

### Prohibited Content Categories

#### Offensive and Adult Content
- Apps containing pornography or depicting sexual acts or sexually explicit material (including nudity)
- Content driving traffic to adult websites
- Material inappropriate for young users without proper safeguards (age gates, content warnings)

#### Illegal Activity
- Apps promoting illegal items, services, or activities
- Developers are responsible for researching compliance with all applicable laws in every jurisdiction where the app is distributed
- Malicious behavior: instructions for hacking, phishing, creating Trojans, or cracking software
- Device rooting tools, DRM circumvention tools, illegal torrents, emulators for unauthorized content

#### Security and Privacy Violations
- Apps that compromise device or user security
- Spyware, malware, or any code that collects data without user consent
- Apps that infringe individual privacy rights or portray people falsely
- Unauthorized use of celebrity images, names, or likeness for commercial purposes

#### Hate and Harmful Content
- Content promoting hatred or discrimination based on protected characteristics
- Content that could cause harm to users or third parties

## Intellectual Property (IP) Policies

### Third-Party IP in Your App
If your app uses IP owned by a third party (licensed characters, brands, music, trademarks):
- You **must** provide documentation of your license or IP rights
- Format: PDF containing formal confirmation letters or signed license agreements
- **Screenshots of licenses are not accepted**
- Submit this documentation during the app submission process

### Unauthorized IP Use
- Unauthorized use of celebrity images or names for commercial purposes is prohibited
- Trademark infringement will result in rejection
- Amazon responds to DMCA takedown requests from rights holders

### Developer IP Warranties
By submitting an app, you warrant that:
- You own or have rights to all content in your app
- Your app contains no infringing material
- Your app contains no malware or malicious code

## Privacy Policy Requirements

### When a Privacy Policy Is Required
A privacy policy is **required** if your app:
- Collects personally identifiable information (PII)
- Uses the device camera, microphone, or location
- Integrates with social networks or third-party accounts
- Includes user-generated content features
- Targets children (COPPA compliance required)

### Privacy Policy Standards
- Must be clearly accessible within the app and/or on your app's store listing
- Must accurately describe what data is collected, how it is used, and with whom it is shared
- Must comply with applicable privacy laws:
  - **GDPR** (EU users)
  - **COPPA** (apps targeting children under 13 in the US)
  - **CCPA** and other state-level privacy laws
  - Local laws in all jurisdictions where the app is distributed

### Data Handling Obligations
Per the Amazon Developer Services Agreement, developers must:
- Obtain user consent before collecting data
- Maintain appropriate security measures for user data
- Comply with GDPR, COPPA, and applicable state privacy regulations

---

## User-Generated Content (UGC) Requirements

Apps that allow user-generated content must include:
- Content filtering mechanisms to prevent policy-violating content
- Age verification systems (where appropriate)
- Mechanisms for users to report offensive content
- Mechanisms for developers to remove offensive content promptly

---

## Gambling and Real Money Transactions

Apps facilitating gambling or competitions with real currency are **permitted** but subject to specific requirements:
- Appropriate safeguards must be in place
- Age verification required
- Geographic restrictions may apply (some jurisdictions prohibit online gambling)
- Must comply with local gambling laws in all target markets

---

## Geographic Restrictions

Amazon may restrict app distribution to specific countries where:
- App content violates local laws
- Content is inappropriate for local cultural norms or sensitivities
- Developer has not complied with region-specific requirements

Developers can also voluntarily restrict their app to specific marketplaces during submission.

---

## App Description Accuracy Policy

App metadata must accurately represent the app:
- Screenshots must show actual app UI (not mockups or competitor screenshots)
- Description must match actual functionality
- Keywords cannot include competitor names or misleading terms
- "Works as outlined in your product description" is an explicit review criterion

---

## Kids and Children's Content

Apps targeting children (Kids category or child-directed apps):
- Must comply with COPPA (US) and equivalent child protection laws
- Cannot collect personal information from children under 13 without verifiable parental consent
- Cannot include behavioral advertising targeted at children
- Must include a privacy policy
- Content must be age-appropriate

## Amazon Developer Services Agreement

Full legal agreement covering all developer obligations:
https://developer.amazon.com/support/legal/da

Key developer obligations under the agreement:
1. Comply with all Program Policies and applicable laws
2. Provide technical support within 24 hours for critical issues; 5 business days for standard requests
3. Deliver and maintain access to all purchased/subscribed IAP content throughout subscription periods
4. Accurately disclose whether IAP items are consumable, subscription-based, or permanent
5. Maintain security measures and comply with data protection laws
