## Connection Requests

- Always include a personalized note (300 character limit).
- Reference specific shared context: a post they wrote, mutual connection, specific company or project.
- Personalized requests achieve ~45% acceptance rate vs. significantly lower for blank requests.
- **Do not** send generic "I'd like to add you to my professional network" requests.

**Example note structure:**
> "Hi [Name], I read your post on [topic] — the point about [specific thing] resonated. I work in [related area] and would love to connect."

## Messaging Channels Compared

| Channel | Who can use | Response rate | Cost |
|---|---|---|---|
| Regular messages | 1st-degree connections only | 25–35% | Free |
| InMail | Anyone (non-connections) | 10–15% | Credits (plan-dependent) |
| Connection request note | Anyone | Higher acceptance → then message free | Free |
| Group messages | Same-group members (not connected) | Varies | Free (see Groups Hack) |
| Voice messages | 1st-degree connections | ~34% higher than text | Free (mobile app only) |

**Rule:** Always prefer a regular message over InMail when the person is already a 1st-degree connection. Build the connection first when possible, then message.

## InMail Best Practices

**Structure:**
1. Why them specifically — reference something from their profile or a post they wrote
2. Concise ask — state clearly what you want
3. Low-commitment close — "Would love your perspective" beats "Can we schedule a call?"

**Length:** Under 300 words. The shorter, the better.

**Credits:** InMail credits are refunded if the recipient responds within 90 days — so thoughtful, targeted InMails conserve budget.

**When InMail makes sense:** Reaching a decision-maker outside your network where a mutual connection or group membership doesn't exist.

## Warm Outreach Strategy

The highest-acceptance-rate connection approach (2025–2026):

1. **Comment on their posts** — leave substantive, insight-adding comments (not just "Great post!") for 1–2 weeks.
2. By the time you send the connection request, your name is familiar and you have demonstrated you're worth connecting with.
3. Send the personalized connection request referencing your prior engagement.

This beats cold InMail or blank connection requests because trust is established before the ask.

**Voice messages** (mobile app only): Use for follow-ups with warm connections. 34% higher response rates than text messages — still novel enough to stand out in 2026.

## LinkedIn Groups Hack

**Non-obvious:** Members of the same LinkedIn Group can message each other directly for free, even if not connected — bypassing InMail entirely.

**How to use it:**
1. Find a group where your target audience is active (search by industry keywords, filter by activity).
2. Join the group.
3. Message group members directly via the group member list.

**Best groups for this:** Niche, high-activity groups with relevant member quality. Avoid large, low-engagement groups where posts get buried.

**Additional value:** Active commenting within a group increases visibility to other members before you reach out.

## Connection Limits & Caps

- **Connection requests:** ~100 per week (enforced since 2021). Hitting this repeatedly risks account restriction. Quality over volume.
- **First-degree connections:** Maximum 30,000. Beyond this limit, new connections become followers only.
- **Followers:** Unlimited — use Creator Mode and consistent content to build a follower base beyond the 30K connection ceiling.
