# Medium Publications

## What Publications Are

Publications are curated collections on Medium — like digital magazines. They have their own follower base, branding, and editorial standards. Publishing in a popular publication exposes your story to that publication's subscribers, not just your own followers.

Benefits of publishing in publications:
- Access to the publication's existing audience
- Boost eligibility via Boost Nomination Program (if publication participates)
- Credibility signal to readers
- Sometimes SEO benefits from established publication domain authority

---

## Roles

| Role | Permissions |
|---|---|
| **Owner** | Full control; can add/remove editors and writers; cannot be removed |
| **Editor** | Can review, edit, publish or reject submitted stories; can add writers; can publish their own stories directly |
| **Writer** | Can submit stories for editor review; cannot self-publish to the publication |

Writers submit → Editors approve/reject → Published under publication banner.

---

## Submitting a Story to a Publication

1. Write or open your draft in Medium editor
2. Click the **3-dot menu** (⋯) in story settings
3. Select **"Submit to publication"**
4. Choose the publication from the list (you must be added as a writer first)
5. Click **"Send for review"**
6. Editor receives notification; they approve, request edits, or reject

Each publication has its own submission guidelines — check the publication's About page or Submission tab before submitting. Guidelines specify: topic focus, formatting preferences, minimum word count, exclusivity requirements.

### Getting Added as a Writer
Publications control their own writer lists. Find the publication's submission page (usually linked from their About section) and follow their process — typically involves pitching or emailing the editor.

Medium maintains a curated list: **"Medium's Huge List of Publications Accepting Submissions"** — searchable in Medium's own lists.

## Creating Your Own Publication

- Each Medium user can own up to **7 publications**
- Create from: your Medium profile → Publications → Create a new publication
- Set: name, description, tags, logo, cover image, custom domain (optional)
- Add writers and editors after creation
- You can submit your own stories or invite contributors

### Publication Settings
- **Layout**: customize the publication homepage
- **Newsletter**: publications have an optional newsletter feature for subscribers
- **Submission guidelines**: set expectations for contributor pitches

---

## Boost Nomination Program (For Publication Editors)

If your publication is accepted into Medium's Boost Nomination Program, you can nominate your best stories for Boost consideration.

**Requirements to remain active in program:**
- Submit at least 1 Boost nomination per month
- Maintain >20% nomination approval rate
- Follow Medium Trust & Safety guidelines

**For writers:** Target publications in the Boost Nomination Program for maximum distribution upside. Medium lists 100+ qualifying publications in the Help Center.

---

## Publication Newsletter

Publications (not individual writers) can send newsletters to their subscribers via Medium's built-in newsletter tool. Subscribers opt in when they follow the publication. This is a key advantage of building or writing for a strong publication — the newsletter list is owned by the publication, not by Medium accounts.

---

## Tips for Working with Publications

- Read the publication's existing stories before submitting — match their tone and depth
- Follow their formatting preferences exactly (some require subheadings; others prefer flowing prose)
- Don't submit simultaneously to multiple publications — exclusivity is typically expected
- A rejection isn't permanent — revise and resubmit, or try a different publication
- Build a relationship with editors over time; consistent quality gets you promoted from writer to editor
